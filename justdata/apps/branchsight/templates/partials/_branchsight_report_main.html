Methods

Data Sources

FDIC Summary of Deposits: This report uses data from the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation (FDIC) Summary of Deposits (SOD). Branch-level data is sourced from the FDIC's annual survey of all FDIC-insured institutions, compiled and maintained in NCRC's curated databases.

Branch Service Types: This analysis includes all branch service types reported in the FDIC Summary of Deposits, including Full Service Branches, Limited Service Branches, Drive-Through Branches, and other branch service types. No service type filtering is applied to the data.

Census Data: Census tract characteristics are sourced from the U.S. Census Bureau American Community Survey (ACS) 5-year estimates.

Data Quality Assurance: All data used in this report has been downloaded, cleaned, and validated by NCRC Research staff to ensure accuracy and reliability.

Definitions

Branch: A unique physical branch location identified using the FDIC unique institution number (uninumbr). Branch counts represent distinct physical locations, not transactions or accounts.

Census Tract Income Categories

Census tract income levels are determined by comparing the tract's median family income to the Area Median Family Income (AMFI):

  • Low Income Tract: Median family income at or below 50% of AMFI
  • Moderate Income Tract: Median family income greater than 50% but at or below 80% of AMFI
  • Middle Income Tract: Median family income greater than 80% but at or below 120% of AMFI
  • Upper Income Tract: Median family income greater than 120% of AMFI
  • Low-to-Moderate Income Census Tract (LMICT or LMI Tract): Census tracts where median family income is at or below 80% of AMFI (combines Low and Moderate income tracts)

Majority-Minority Census Tract (MMCT): Census tracts where minority populations represent more than 50% of the total population, as defined by the U.S. Census Bureau.

Branch Category Classifications (Mutually Exclusive)

  • LMI Only Branches: Branches located in Low-to-Moderate Income Census Tracts that are not Majority-Minority Census Tracts
  • MMCT Only Branches: Branches located in Majority-Minority Census Tracts that are not Low-to-Moderate Income Census Tracts
  • Both LMICT/MMCT Branches: Branches located in census tracts that are both Low-to-Moderate Income and Majority-Minority
  • Other Branches: Branches that are neither LMICT nor MMCT

Note: Each branch is counted in only one category based on its census tract characteristics.

2020 Census Boundary Changes: The 2020 census boundaries that took effect in 2022 resulted in a 30% increase in the number of majority-minority census tracts nationally. The vast majority of these newly designated majority-minority tracts are not low-to-moderate income tracts. When analyzing changes between 2021 and 2022, this census boundary update should be considered as it may create apparent shifts that reflect statistical reclassification rather than actual changes.

Deposits: Total deposits held at each branch location, expressed in millions of dollars, as reported in the FDIC Summary of Deposits.

Calculations

Branch Counts: Unique branches are identified using the unique institution number (uninumbr). Branch counts represent distinct physical branch locations.

Net Change: Calculated as the difference between branch counts in the first year and the final year of the analysis period.

Percentage of Total Branches: Calculated as (branches in category / total branches) × 100.

Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI)

A standard measure of market concentration used by regulators including the Department of Justice and Federal Trade Commission. HHI is calculated as the sum of squared market shares of all banks in a market:

HHI = Σ(market sharei)2

Market shares are expressed as percentages (0-100), so HHI ranges from 0 to 10,000. Lower values indicate more competitive markets with many banks. Higher values indicate concentrated markets dominated by few banks.

Concentration Levels:

  • HHI < 1,500: Low concentration (competitive market)
  • HHI 1,500-2,500: Moderate concentration
  • HHI > 2,500: High concentration

Note on HHI Calculation: The HHI calculation uses deposit data from all banks in the study area, not just the banks shown in the report tables.

Geographic Identifiers: Counties are identified using geoid5, a 5-digit code where the first 2 digits represent the state FIPS code and the next 3 digits represent the county FIPS code.

Abbreviations

  • FDIC: Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation
  • FIPS: Federal Information Processing Standards
  • HHI: Herfindahl-Hirschman Index
  • HUD: U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development
  • LMI: Low-to-Moderate Income
  • LMICT: Low-to-Moderate Income Census Tract
  • MMCT: Majority-Minority Census Tract
  • SOD: Summary of Deposits
