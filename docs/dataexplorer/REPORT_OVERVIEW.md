# DataExplorer Report Overview

This document provides a simple, plain English explanation of what each section and table shows in the Area Analysis and Lender Analysis reports.

---

## Area Analysis Report

The Area Analysis Report shows mortgage lending activity and housing characteristics for a selected geographic area (counties or metro areas). It helps you understand who is getting loans, where they live, and how the housing market looks in that area.

### Section 1: Population Demographics

This section shows basic information about the people and housing in the selected area, plus overall loan activity.

**Table 1: Population Demographics (Adult Population 18+)**
- **What it shows:** A chart showing the racial and ethnic makeup of the adult population (age 18 and older) in the selected area
- **Time periods:** Shows data for 2010, 2020, and the most recent available year
- **Why it matters:** Helps you understand the demographic context of the area where loans are being made

**Table 2: Loans by Loan Purpose Over Time**
- **What it shows:** A chart showing how many loans were made each year, broken down by loan purpose (Home Purchase, Refinance, Home Equity)
- **Time periods:** Shows all selected years
- **Why it matters:** Shows trends in lending activity - are people buying homes, refinancing, or taking out home equity loans?

**Table 3: Loan Amounts by Loan Purpose Over Time**
- **What it shows:** A chart showing the total dollar amount of loans made each year, broken down by loan purpose
- **Time periods:** Shows all selected years
- **Why it matters:** Shows whether loan amounts are increasing or decreasing over time, and which loan purposes involve the most money

**Table 4: Housing Costs and Burden**
- **What it shows:** A table showing key housing cost measures: median household income, median home value, median monthly owner costs, owner cost burden (what percentage of income goes to housing), median rent, and rental burden
- **Time periods:** Shows data for 2006-2010, 2016-2020, and the most recent ACS period
- **Why it matters:** Helps you understand housing affordability in the area - are people spending too much of their income on housing?

**Table 5: Owner Occupancy Rates**
- **What it shows:** A table showing what percentage of households own their homes (versus rent), both overall and broken down by race and ethnicity
- **Time periods:** Shows data for 2006-2010, 2016-2020, and the most recent ACS period
- **Why it matters:** Shows homeownership rates and whether there are differences in homeownership by race/ethnicity

**Table 6: Housing Units**
- **What it shows:** A table showing the total number of housing units, what percentage are 1-4 unit buildings (single-family, duplexes, small apartment buildings), what percentage are manufactured/mobile homes, what percentage are large apartment buildings (5+ units), and what percentage of 1-4 unit homes are owner-occupied
- **Time periods:** Shows data for 2006-2010, 2016-2020, and the most recent ACS period
- **Why it matters:** Shows the types of housing available in the area and how much is owner-occupied versus rental

### Section 2: Loans by Category (Most Recent 5 Years)

This section shows detailed breakdowns of loans made in the selected area over the most recent 5 years. You can filter by loan purpose (All Loans, Home Purchase, Refinance, or Home Equity).

**Table 1: Loans by Race and Ethnicity**
- **What it shows:** A table showing what percentage of loans went to borrowers of different races and ethnicities each year
- **Time periods:** Most recent 5 years
- **Why it matters:** Shows whether loans are being made fairly across different racial and ethnic groups
- **Note:** Only shows race/ethnicity categories that represent at least 1% of total loans in any year

**Table 2: Loans by Borrower Income**
- **What it shows:** A table showing what percentage of loans went to borrowers in different income categories (Low & Moderate Income, Low Income, Moderate Income, Middle Income, Upper Income) each year
- **Time periods:** Most recent 5 years
- **Why it matters:** Shows whether loans are being made to low- and moderate-income borrowers
- **Note:** Includes a "Population Share" column showing what percentage of the area's population falls into each income category (for comparison)

**Table 3: Loans by Neighborhood Income**
- **What it shows:** A table showing what percentage of loans went to neighborhoods (census tracts) in different income categories each year
- **Time periods:** Most recent 5 years
- **Why it matters:** Shows whether loans are being made in low- and moderate-income neighborhoods
- **Note:** Includes a "Population Share" column showing what percentage of census tracts fall into each income category

**Table 4: Loans by Neighborhood Demographics**
- **What it shows:** A table showing what percentage of loans went to neighborhoods with different racial and ethnic compositions (Majority Minority, High Minority, Moderate Minority, Middle Minority, Low Minority) each year
- **Time periods:** Most recent 5 years
- **Why it matters:** Shows whether loans are being made in minority neighborhoods
- **Note:** Includes a "Population Share" column showing what percentage of the population lives in each type of neighborhood

**Table 5: Loan Costs**
- **What it shows:** A table showing average loan costs (interest rate, total loan costs, origination charges) each year
- **Time periods:** Most recent 5 years
- **Why it matters:** Shows whether loan costs are fair and whether they're changing over time

### Section 3: Top Lenders Analysis

This section shows detailed breakdowns for the top 10 lenders in the selected area. Each table shows lenders as rows and metrics as columns. You can filter by loan purpose (All Loans, Home Purchase, Refinance, or Home Equity).

**Table 1: Loans by Race and Ethnicity by Lender**
- **What it shows:** A table showing what percentage of each lender's loans went to borrowers of different races and ethnicities
- **Why it matters:** Shows how different lenders compare in terms of serving borrowers of different races and ethnicities
- **Note:** Only shows race/ethnicity categories that represent at least 1% of total loans across all lenders

**Table 2: Loans by Borrower Income by Lender**
- **What it shows:** A table showing what percentage of each lender's loans went to borrowers in different income categories
- **Why it matters:** Shows how different lenders compare in terms of serving low- and moderate-income borrowers

**Table 3: Loans by Neighborhood Income by Lender**
- **What it shows:** A table showing what percentage of each lender's loans went to neighborhoods in different income categories
- **Why it matters:** Shows how different lenders compare in terms of lending in low- and moderate-income neighborhoods

**Table 4: Loans by Neighborhood Demographics by Lender**
- **What it shows:** A table showing what percentage of each lender's loans went to neighborhoods with different racial and ethnic compositions
- **Why it matters:** Shows how different lenders compare in terms of lending in minority neighborhoods

**Table 5: Loan Costs by Lender**
- **What it shows:** A table showing average loan costs (interest rate, total loan costs, origination charges) for each lender
- **Why it matters:** Shows how different lenders compare in terms of loan costs
- **Note:** You can filter by lender type (All, Banks, Mortgage Companies, Credit Unions)

### Section 4: Market Concentration (HHI)

**Table 1: HHI Market Concentration**
- **What it shows:** A chart showing the Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI) for the mortgage market in the selected area, broken down by loan purpose
- **What HHI means:** HHI measures market concentration. Higher numbers mean the market is more concentrated (fewer lenders control more of the market). Lower numbers mean the market is more competitive (more lenders share the market)
- **Time periods:** Most recent 5 years
- **Why it matters:** Shows whether the mortgage market is competitive or dominated by a few large lenders

## Lender Analysis Report

The Lender Analysis Report shows how a specific lender performs compared to peer lenders in a selected geographic area. It helps you understand whether a lender is serving the community fairly and how they compare to other lenders.

### Section 1: Lender Performance Over Time

This section shows how the selected lender's loan activity has changed over time.

**Table 1: Loans by Loan Purpose Over Time**
- **What it shows:** A chart showing how many loans the lender made each year, broken down by loan purpose (Home Purchase, Refinance, Home Equity)
- **Time periods:** Shows all selected years
- **Why it matters:** Shows trends in the lender's lending activity - are they making more or fewer loans over time?

**Table 2: Loan Amounts by Loan Purpose Over Time**
- **What it shows:** A chart showing the total dollar amount of loans the lender made each year, broken down by loan purpose
- **Time periods:** Shows all selected years
- **Why it matters:** Shows whether the lender's loan amounts are increasing or decreasing over time

**Table 3: Top Metros by Loan Purpose**
- **What it shows:** A table showing which metro areas (CBSAs) the lender made the most loans in, broken down by loan purpose
- **Time periods:** Shows all selected years
- **Why it matters:** Shows where the lender is most active geographically
- **Note:** Only shown when the geography scope is not "Custom CBSA"

### Section 2: Lender Performance Comparison

This section compares the selected lender to a comparison group (Peer Lenders, All Other Lenders, Banks Only, Mortgage Companies Only, or Credit Unions Only). Each table shows the lender's performance and the comparison group's performance side-by-side. You can filter by loan purpose (All Loans, Home Purchase, Refinance, or Home Equity).

**Table 1: Loans by Race and Ethnicity**
- **What it shows:** A table comparing what percentage of the lender's loans went to borrowers of different races and ethnicities versus the comparison group
- **Time periods:** Most recent 3 years
- **Why it matters:** Shows whether the lender is serving borrowers of different races and ethnicities at similar rates to other lenders
- **Note:** Only shows race/ethnicity categories that represent at least 1% of total loans

**Table 2: Loans by Borrower Income**
- **What it shows:** A table comparing what percentage of the lender's loans went to borrowers in different income categories versus the comparison group
- **Time periods:** Most recent 3 years
- **Why it matters:** Shows whether the lender is serving low- and moderate-income borrowers at similar rates to other lenders

**Table 3: Loans by Neighborhood Income**
- **What it shows:** A table comparing what percentage of the lender's loans went to neighborhoods in different income categories versus the comparison group
- **Time periods:** Most recent 3 years
- **Why it matters:** Shows whether the lender is lending in low- and moderate-income neighborhoods at similar rates to other lenders

**Table 4: Loans by Neighborhood Demographics**
- **What it shows:** A table comparing what percentage of the lender's loans went to neighborhoods with different racial and ethnic compositions versus the comparison group
- **Time periods:** Most recent 3 years
- **Why it matters:** Shows whether the lender is lending in minority neighborhoods at similar rates to other lenders

**Table 5: Loan Costs**
- **What it shows:** A table comparing the lender's average loan costs (interest rate, total loan costs, origination charges) versus the comparison group
- **Time periods:** Most recent 3 years
- **Why it matters:** Shows whether the lender's loan costs are similar to or different from other lenders

## Key Terms

**Borrower Income:** The income of the person taking out the loan, categorized as Low & Moderate Income (LMI), Low Income, Moderate Income, Middle Income, or Upper Income based on area median income.

**Neighborhood Income:** The income level of the census tract (neighborhood) where the property is located, categorized based on area median income.

**Neighborhood Demographics:** The racial and ethnic composition of the census tract (neighborhood) where the property is located, categorized as Majority Minority, High Minority, Moderate Minority, Middle Minority, or Low Minority.

**Loan Purpose:** The reason for the loan - Home Purchase (buying a home), Refinance (refinancing an existing mortgage), or Home Equity (taking out a home equity loan or line of credit).

**Owner Cost Burden:** The percentage of a homeowner's income that goes toward housing costs (mortgage, taxes, insurance, utilities). Generally, if this is over 30%, the homeowner is considered "cost burdened."

**Rental Burden:** The percentage of a renter's income that goes toward rent and utilities. Generally, if this is over 30%, the renter is considered "cost burdened."

**HHI (Herfindahl-Hirschman Index):** A measure of market concentration. It ranges from 0 to 10,000. Higher numbers mean fewer lenders control more of the market. Generally, HHI below 1,500 is considered competitive, 1,500-2,500 is moderately concentrated, and above 2,500 is highly concentrated.
